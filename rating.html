Modern Rock Climbing

How do climbers know what to climb?

To ensure climbers can choose routes that match their abilities and preferences, various rating systems have been developed to provide valuable information about the difficulty and characteristics of climbing routes.

  • Yosemite Decimal System (YDS)

    Grade Range: 5.0 - 5.15.

    The Yosemite Decimal System is one of the oldest and most widely used climbing rating systems in North America. It rates climbs based on difficulty and danger.

  • French Grading System

    Grade Range: 1 - 9b+

    The French grading system is commonly used in Europe and has been adopted worldwide. It focuses on the technical difficulty of the climb without considering other factors. The grades are denoted by numbers, with higher numbers indicating greater difficulty.

  • V-Scale (Bouldering)

    Grade Range: V0 - V17

    The V-Scale is specifically designed for bouldering and rates problems based on difficulty. It is a numerical system with increasing numbers signifying greater challenge.

  • UIAA Grading System (Alpine Climbing)

    Grade Range: I - VII+

    The UIAA grading system is commonly used for alpine climbing and emphasizes the overall difficulty, commitment level, and objective hazards of a climb. It includes technical, physical, and psychological factors.
