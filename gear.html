Modern Rock Climbing

Gear and Equipment

  • Rope:

    Ropes are dynamic and designed to stretch to absorb the impact of a falling climber

  • Harness:

    A device made out of strong webbing that typically has a padded waist belt and two leg loops, connected in front and back; it is used to secure a rope to a climber.

  • Helmet:

    A piece of safety equipment that primarily protects the skull of a climber against falling debris (such as rocks or dropped pieces of protection) and impact forces during a fall.

  • Climbing shoe:

    Typical climbing shoes have a close fit, little if any padding, and a smooth, sticky rubber sole with an extended rubber rand

  • Belay Device:

    A belay device acts as a brake on the climbing rope by applying friction to it. The device, plus the belayer's quick "braking hand" (which locks off the free end of the rope), helps keep tension on the rope and helps protect the climber at the other end.

  • "Protection" :

    Gear that connects the rope to a rock face to protect the climber in the event of a fall
